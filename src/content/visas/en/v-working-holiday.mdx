## Who can apply for this type of visa?

Working holiday programs, also known as youth mobility or cultural exchange programs, offer young individuals the opportunity to travel and work in a foreign country for an extended period. These programs facilitate a unique blend of work experience, cultural immersion, and personal growth. Participants can engage in a wide range of jobs, such as hospitality, agriculture, tourism, or retail, allowing them to earn money while experiencing the local culture and lifestyle. Working holiday programs foster cross-cultural understanding, language development, and the formation of lifelong friendships with people from different backgrounds. It's an adventure that enables young individuals to broaden their horizons, gain valuable skills, and create lasting memories while exploring a new country.

The Working holiday Visa in Colombia is open to young applicants from the age 18 to 30 (included), who are nationals of countries with which Colombia has a current working holiday agreement. These contries are:

## Requirements

Besides the [general requirements](https://www.notion.so/General-visa-application-rules-1dc2c565143e4adb95086f993f184e36?pvs=21), you will need:

### 1. Reason for Applying (Motivation Letter)

Write a letter explaining why you're applying for the visa, confirming you haven't been part of the Program before, and that you're between eighteen (18) and thirty (30) years old.

### 2. Agreement to Terms (Commitment form)

Complete the Ministry of Foreign Affairs' commitment form, available on [their website](https://www.cancilleria.gov.co/sites/default/files/FOTOS2020/dp-fo-155_compromiso_de_respetar_la_finalidad_del_acuerdo_vacaciones_y_trabajo_v2_1_0.pdf).

### 3. Proof of Return Journey

Provide proof that you've booked your journey back home.

### 4. No Criminal History (Criminal Records)

Show a clean criminal record from any country you've lived in for the last three years, with the record legalized for Colombia and translated into Spanish.

### 5. Health Insurance Coverage

Ensure you have health insurance that covers all sorts of emergencies in Colombia for the duration of your stay. It should be in Spanish or translated. [Genki](https://genki.world/?with=capitaleme) is a recommended option, though we're not affiliated with them. It's valid for two years, affordable, can be canceled anytime, and you can get it quickly online.

### 6. Financial Stability Proof (Bank Statements)

Show bank statements from the last three months proving you have at least five times the current legal monthly minimum wage or the amount specified by the Agreement. As of 2023, the minimum wage in Colombia is approximately $281 USD.

### 7. Sponsorship Letter (if needed)

If someone is sponsoring your stay, provide a letter from them promising to cover your expenses and ensure your departure from Colombia, along with proof of their financial capability.
