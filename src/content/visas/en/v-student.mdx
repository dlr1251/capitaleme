## Am I eligible for this visa?

if you’re enrolled in an academic program with an educational institution recognized by the Colombian government, you’re eligible to apply for this visa. This includes all the academic levels from elementary school up to post-grade / doctorate studies.

## What are its benefits and restrictions?

### **Validity depends on the length of the program**

It allows you to stay in Colombia during the length of the programm, for up to two (2) years.

### **No beneficiaries except for post-graduate students**

It does not allow you to bring your spouse or kids under 25, unless you are a postgraduate student.

### **Work permit only for post-graduate students**

If you are enrolled in a postgraduate program, such as a Master, a Ph.D. or similar, you will be allowed to work for Colombian companies up to twenty (20) hours per week, provided that your employer registers your job offer before Migración Colombia.

### **No time accrue towards residency**

Since it is a visitor-type (V) visa, it won't allow you to add time in order to apply for a residency or (R) visa.

## What do I need to apply?

In order to apply for this visa, you will need to show evidence that you have been accepted on a educational program and that you have enough financial solvency to live in the country.

The specific requirements for a student visa may vary depending on your sponsorship. If you are applying **with the sponsorship of a Colombian host**, you will need to provide documentation of your sponsor's financial solvency and the sponsor’s motivational letter. 

If you are applying with **the sponsorship of an educational institution**, they will present the application on your behalf. 

If you are applying **on your own behalf**, you will need to provide documentation of your own financial solvency and a motivational letter.

### **Applying on your own behalf**

#### **Motivational letter**
    
    You will need to write a motivational letter informing your full identification (full legal name, passport number, birthdate, nationality and residency), explaining thoroughly and accurately the activities that you are planning to do under the student visa, including the name of the school, the address of the school, the type and name of the program/course you will attending,  its schedule, the place where you will be staying and mention and give details from some contacts in the school.
    
#### **Proof of your financial solvency**
    
    Provide your bank statements from the previous six months, showing a monthly income of at least 10 Colombian minimum wages, that is $14,235,000 COP per month.
    
    *(As of 2025, the minimum wage in Colombia is $1,423,500 per month)*
    
#### **Admission certificate**
    
    Certificate of admission or enrollment in the educational institution indicating the degree or program for which the student is admitted or enrolled and its duration.
    
#### A valid **health insurance**

### Sponsored by a **Colombian or foreigner host**

You can get your visa application sponsored or backed up by a Colombian national or Colombian resident (a foreigner with a current valid Resident visa), if this is the case, you will need:

#### **Letter from your sponsor**
    
    On this letter, your sponsor confirms that he/she will take full responsibility for your personal and financial well being during your stay in Colombia, including covering all expenses related to transportation, accommodation and repatriation.
    
    The sponsor needs to include his/her phone number, physical address, email address, the type of relationship with you (relative, fiance, friend, business partner, etc.), the manual  signature (on pen) and a fingerprint (one fingerprint) of either the right or left index finger, or right or left thumb finger.
    
    The sponsor also commits on this letter to inform the Colombian government if you stay in the country illegally and to ensure your return to your country of origin. Ignoring this rule may result in sanctions.‍ The sponsor also needs to state in this letter previously sponsored visa applications.
    
#### **Proof of the sponsor's financial solvency**
    
    Attach his/her bank statements from the previous six (6) months, showing monthly income of at least 10 Colombian minimum wages.
