Who can apply for this visa?

Foreigners aspiring to work in Colombia with a legal entity through an employment contract

What are the benefits and restrictions for this visa?

Up to three years validity

This visa allows you to live and work in Colombia for up to three years. The time granted will be up to the authorities.

This visa admits beneficiaries

With this visa you will be able to bring your family with you as beneficiaries. That is, you can bring your spouse and/or children to live with you, once the main visa is approved.

Restricted work permit

Please notice that this type of visa only allow you to work for a specific Colombian company. If you are planning on changing jobs, or get fired, you must report that to the Ministry of Foreign Relations

Time accrue towards residency

You can apply for an R-visa if you accumulate five continous years holding this visa without losing it.

Requirements

1. Contract Summary Document

Provide a summarized version of the contract as specified by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, available on their website. It must be signed by both the legal representative of the company hiring and the foreign worker. This document should clearly outline the key details of the work activity in Colombia.

2. Employer’s Motivation Letter

The employer should write a letter explaining why the foreign worker is suitable for the job. It should detail the role, the worker’s qualifications, why a Colombian citizen isn’t being hired, and how many jobs the company is creating directly.

3. Financial Proof from Employer

The employer must show bank statements or financial certifications from the last four months. These should prove an average monthly income at least one hundred times the current legal monthly minimum wage.

4. Qualification Proof for Unregulated Professions

If the job isn’t regulated, provide an apostilled (or legalized and translated) copy of the professional degree or certifications of work experience that prove the foreign worker’s qualifications.

5. Permit for Regulated Professions

If the job is regulated, both the foreign worker and the hiring company must get the necessary permit or license from the appropriate Professional Council. This must be presented when applying for second or subsequent visas.

If the hiring entity is a Colombian state organ, institution, or entity, a Governmental International Organization, Diplomatic Mission, or Consular Office accredited in Colombia, only a visa request letter signed by the legal representative or the person acting on their behalf will be required. If the hiring entity is a mixed economy company in which private capital exceeds public capital, it must meet all the requirements established for this type of visa.
